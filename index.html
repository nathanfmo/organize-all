A guide to note-taking methods

Note-taking is an effective practice for retaining and reviewing what you've learned. Whether you'd like to improve on your current system or learn your first system, this guide is for you.

Background

When I first entered high school, I had gotten along fine without taking any notes. I had no idea what "studying" even referred to outside of doing homework and projects. Running smack-dab into the wall that was my first difficult class was a wake-up call with consequences in the form of bad grades.
I knew things had to change, and so I went about researching what it was to study and learn effectively. One of the biggest changes I experimented with was note-taking. I went through a lot of iterations of note-taking systems, and I recorded the pros and cons of each. It was never my intention to share this information with anyone outside of my organization mentees, but it's a resource that will hopefully will help you in the same way it has helped me!
